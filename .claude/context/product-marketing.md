# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-05*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Professional video production studio using AI tools — not an AI tool you're using yourself.

**What it does:** FlarePix produces product photography, product video, AI imagery, and AI video for Amazon and ecommerce sellers. The studio combines real footage filming with AI-assisted post-production (hybrid approach), delivering cinematic-quality output at a fraction of full-production cost. All output is human-reviewed before delivery.

**Product category:** Professional video and photography production services for ecommerce/Amazon sellers.

**Product type:** Service business (not SaaS, not product). B2B.

**Business model:** Project-based quoting (customized per project — product type, volume, content type, timeline). No fixed pricing packages.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Amazon sellers need professional visual content to convert listings, but existing options are either too expensive and slow (full production studios) or too generic and damaging to brand perception (self-serve AI tools).

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Full-production studios: Excellent quality but $5,000–$50,000+ and 6–12 week lead times. Prohibitive for sellers launching multiple products per quarter.
- Self-serve AI video tools: Fast and cheap, but output looks generic and "AI" — savvy consumers recognize it, which hurts brand credibility rather than building it.
- Freelance platforms: Quality inconsistency, communication overhead, no systematic QC.
- Template studios: One-size-fits-all output, no differentiation from competitors.

**What it costs them:**
- Time: Weeks spent finding, vetting, and managing vendors
- Money: Either overspending on full production or wasting budget on AI output that doesn't convert
- Opportunity: Poor visual content = lower conversion rate = lost sales on Amazon

**Emotional tension:**
- Fear: "Will this look right? Will it look AI? Will it hurt my brand?"
- Frustration: "I don't have time to manage yet another vendor overseas"
- Doubt: "Is this worth the investment? Will it actually convert?"
- Overwhelm: "I don't know what's right for my product — AI video or real filming?"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**
- **productvideostudio.com** — Traditional video production studio. No AI capability, no differentiation, weak SEO for `amazon product video production`. Falls short because: positioned as generic studio, not Amazon-specific.
- **neverframe.com** — AI-first video production. Real filming capability is unclear. Falls short because: hybrid claim without transparent real-footage offering, AI output quality inconsistent.
- **vidico.com** — Professional video studio but not Amazon-focused. Falls short because: generic positioning, higher pricing without Amazon-specific expertise.

**Secondary competitors (self-serve AI video tools):**
- **hi-light.ai** — Markets itself as "one-click AI video." Falls short because: the one-click output is visibly generic and recognized as AI-generated by consumers. Misleading positioning — actual quality requires professional intervention.
- **Other AI video platforms** (runway, pika, synthesia) — Self-serve tools that teams must operate themselves. Falls short because: require internal expertise to get good output; no human QC layer; output looks same as competitors'.

**Indirect competitors:**
- In-house content teams (dedicated video staff). Falls short because: expensive, single-purpose, no external perspective.
- DIY smartphone recording. Falls short because: obviously amateur, poor conversion performance on Amazon.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Hybrid production model: Real footage + AI post-production (not AI-only, not full-production-only)
- Human QC on every output: Every video and image is reviewed by a professional before delivery
- Amazon and ecommerce focus: Specific expertise in what converts on Amazon listings vs. generic video production
- Manufacturing hub location: Based in Qingdao, China (world's manufacturing hub) — works naturally with sellers who source from Chinese manufacturers
- Transparent about AI limitations: Publicly acknowledges that AI video output without professional oversight produces generic, identifiable AI content

**How we do it differently:**
- We position AI as a production tool we operate professionally, not a self-serve product we hand to clients
- Every project gets a dedicated creative brief and human quality review before delivery
- Our hybrid workflow (real filming + AI post) is transparent — we explain what we shoot vs. what we generate

**Why that's better:**
- Client gets cinematic quality at 30-50% of full-production cost
- Turnaround fits product launch calendars (2–5 business days for AI video, 2–4 weeks for brand film)
- No "AI look" or generic output — every piece is reviewed against brand brief

**Why customers choose us:**
- They want professional quality without full-production prices
- They've tried self-serve AI tools and been disappointed by the generic output
- They need a team that understands Amazon listing requirements (A+ content specs, video format requirements, conversion optimization)
